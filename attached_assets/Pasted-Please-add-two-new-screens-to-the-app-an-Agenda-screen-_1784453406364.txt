## UI/UX Requirements (apply to both screens)

**Structure**
- Agenda and Breakout Sessions must be two **separate, distinct screens** (not combined into one), each reachable directly from the main navigation — don't bury one inside the other beyond the one link from the Agenda's breakout row mentioned above
- Easy to move between them and back to the rest of the app (chat channels, admin dashboard) without getting stuck — a persistent bottom nav bar or top tab bar is ideal if the app doesn't already have one

**Search & Filter**
- Add a search bar on the Breakout Sessions screen so someone can type a keyword (e.g. "Joule" or "Concur") and see every matching session across all tracks, regardless of which tab/track it's in
- Add a filter to show "What's happening right now" based on the current time — genuinely useful for someone standing in the venue trying to decide where to go next
- On the Agenda screen, filtering isn't essential (it's one linear timeline), but make it easy to jump to "right now," e.g. auto-scroll to or highlight the current/next agenda item when opened

**Visual design**
- Color-code by track/category on the Breakout Sessions screen — Demopods, Tech Talk, Design Thinking Zones, MCC Walkthrough, Ask an Expert Corner, Support Accreditation Zone, and the separate Demo Pods (Experience Center) track should each have a distinct, consistent color used for their tab/badge/accent throughout
- Should look clean, modern, and professional — this represents the event, not just an internal tool, so put real design effort in (matching the same design bar as the rest of the app, respecting dark mode if that's already been added)
- Prioritize readability and scanability over density — people will glance at this quickly while walking, not sit and study it

**Nice extra features, if time allows (not required, but good if easy to add)**
- A "Happening Now" or "Up Next" highlight/badge on whichever agenda item or session is currently active, updating live as time passes
- Ability to bookmark/star a specific breakout session as a personal reminder
- A subtle "time until this starts" countdown on the next upcoming agenda item

Please don't let visual polish or extra features come at the cost of getting the actual content (all tracks, times, locations above) fully correct — accuracy first, then make it look great.
